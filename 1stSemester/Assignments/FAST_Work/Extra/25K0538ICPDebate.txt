Main Points:

1. Digital Ceiling and Career Sabotage.
While the world moves toward AI and 5G, Pakistan is implementing "Firewalls" that have degraded internet speeds by 30-40%.
Major freelancing platforms like Fiverr have started flagging Pakistani accounts as "unavailable" due to unreliable connectivity. This effectively kills the "Gig Economy" which was the one safety net for Pakistani youth.
The IT sector, often touted as the future, is suffering losses estimated at $300 million due to these disruptions.
Abroad, digital infrastructure is a basic utility, not a luxury. You cannot compete globally with one hand tied behind your back by state censorship.

2. The Mathematical Impossibility of Asset Ownership.
The "Pakistani Dream" of owning a home and a car is mathematically impossible for an honest salaried professional in the current economy.
In Lahore, a standard 10-marla house now costs roughly PKR 60 million (6 Crore). The average monthly salary is approximately PKR 82,100. Even if you save 100% of your salary (impossible), it would take 60+ years to buy a house.
A Toyota Corolla, a standard middle-class car, costs over PKR 7.7 million. In the UK, a similar vehicle costs a fraction of the annual average salary. In Pakistan, it costs nearly 8-10 years of the average total salary. 
Staying in Pakistan doesn't mean "compromising on luxuries"; it means accepting a lifetime of renting and inability to build generational wealth for a comfortable future of your children.

3. The Rigged System by the Elite.
Hard work in Pakistan does not translate to success because the economy is rigged by the Elite.
The UNDP reports that $17.4 billion (roughly 6% of the economy) is siphoned off annually by the elite (feudals, corporate sector, powerful lobbies) through privileges and tax breaks.
The government issues Statutory Regulatory Orders (SROs) to protect specific industries (like autos and sugar) from competition, costing the public billions while delivering inferior products.
Asking to stay in Pakistan is essentially asking students to stay and play a game where the rules are written against them. Moving abroad offers a level playing field where "merit", not "connections" determine their success.

4. Terrible Enviroment for Health.
You cannot build a future if you are physically dying faster. "Opportunity" is irrelevant if you don't have the health to enjoy it.
According to the Air Quality Life Index (AQLI) 2024, air pollution is cutting the life expectancy of an average Pakistani by 3.3 to 4 years. In Lahore and Peshawar, residents are losing up to 7 years of their lives.
Lahore met WHO air quality standards on only 7 days over the last 5 years.

5. A person has the right to pursue the best possible life.
A country exists for the people and to make lives easier for its people, not the other way around, where the people are the ones serving the country by compromising on their quality of life, their children's education, their enviroment, their career, but the country doesnt reciprocate anything or the very bare minimum back to its people, or very often making irrational decisions that harm its people.
If a citizen permanently moves abroad for: Better safety, Stronger rule of law, Higher living standards, Quality education for children, Job security, That is a completely valid and rational decision and is every person's right.

6. Permanently moving abroad doesn’t erase identity or connection.
People who settle abroad permanently often:
Support family back home
Help relatives migrate through legal channels
Contribute to Pakistan from a stable environment
Maintain cultural ties
Advocate for Pakistan abroad and spread its culture and historical knowledge.
Permanent relocation doesn't mean “cutting ties", It simply means living in a system where one’s rights and future are secure.

7. Pakistan cannot rely on guilt or emotional appeals to retain people.
If a country wants people to stay, it must offer:
Merit-based systems
Stable employment
Predictable governance
Safety
Good healthcare
Strong education

If these incentives are lacking, it is unfair to expect individuals to sacrifice their lives “for national interest.”

8. Your first responsibility is to your future children
Parents want:
Better schools
Cleaner environments
Equal opportunities
Safer childhood
Higher chances of success

If a system cannot guarantee these, permanent relocation becomes a protective decision for the next generation, not an escape.

9. Staying in a struggling system doesn’t automatically “help the nation”
Permanent residency abroad does not:
Harm Pakistan’s economy
Harm national security
Harm institutions

And one person staying behind does not magically fix:
Corruption
Inflation
Weak infrastructure
Limited job markets

“A country improves through structural reforms, not through individuals sacrificing their personal ambitions.”

Defense Points:

1. something along the lines of: "You should stay and work for the national interest." or "Moving abroad hurts Pakistan's economy, not giving it any space to improve".
 The greatest service to the national interest is to become an economic asset, not a liability. 
A struggling graduate in Pakistan adds to the unemployment statistics. A successful professional abroad keeps this country afloat.
Fact: Fact: Remittances from overseas Pakistanis reached $30.3 billion in FY24, which is more than our total exports and far exceeds Foreign Direct Investment. The diaspora is the only reason Pakistan has not defaulted. Leaving is helping far more than staying.

2.  something along the lines of: "The education system here is sufficient, and we have vast manpower and youth", or anything calling the education system "good enough".
We have a quantity of youth, not quality of opportunity. Our degrees are losing value globally because of the education emergency and outdated curriculums.
Fact: Pakistan has 26 million out-of-school children. Our universities are struggling to break into the top 500 global rankings. Employers abroad and even locally prefer international graduates because local curricula lack essential employability skills. Staying often means receiving an education that leaves you unprepared for the global market.

3. something along the lines of: "Moving abroad is abandoning the country" and/or "leaving others to stay back and struggle rather than supporting them".
Leaving the country and starting a business or start-up outside is more often beneficial to Pakistan rather than abandoning it or leaving others to struggle.
Look at the startup ecosystem. Venture Capital firms like i2i Ventures and Indus Valley Capital are run by Pakistanis who went abroad, succeeded, and brought that money and knowledge back to Pakistan. They could only help Pakistan because they left first. It would have never happened if they stayed.

4. something along the lines of: "Living or moving abraod permanently is selfish"
What IS truly selfish: expecting individuals to stay in a difficult environment just to satisfy emotional arguments.
Choosing safety, opportunity, and stability is a human right.

5. something along the lines of: "Pakistan needs its youth, don’t leave permanently."
Countries improve through policy, governance, and systems. Not through guilt tripping individuals. One person staying or leaving doesn’t determine national progress.
